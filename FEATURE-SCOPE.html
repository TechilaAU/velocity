The agreed scope

One platform to run the entire dealership

This document is the full inventory of features Velocity One will deliver — the single source of truth for what is in scope. It follows the complete journey of the dealership: a vehicle arriving in stock, a customer enquiry, the negotiation, the deal and its finances, the NZ compliance paperwork signed on the spot, delivery, servicing, and the reporting that ties it all together — then opens it all up through an open developer API and a built-in CRM. The platform is designed demo-first, so every data-driven feature works on demonstration data from day one and connects to each live New Zealand data source the moment its credentials are provisioned.

International expansion

One platform, three markets — NZ, then Australia & the US

Velocity One launches in New Zealand, but it is built market-agnostic from the ground up. Everything that differs between countries — tax, legal disclosures, vehicle-data sources, registration forms and listing channels — lives in a pluggable localisation layer. Expanding to a new country means swapping that layer for the market, not rebuilding the application. The core deal engine, CRM, stock, e-sign and API are identical everywhere.

Expansion roadmap

Now · Market 1
New Zealand — launch & prove

Ship the full platform, win NZ dealers, and harden the localisation layer against a real, regulated market.

Next · Market 2
Australia — the natural second market

Build the AU compliance pack & tax engine (10% GST + stamp duty), connect PPSR, CarHistory & carsales.com.au, add state dealer-licensing fields, and localise the point-of-sale documents. Closest market, shared language, biggest near-term opportunity.

Then · Market 3
United States — scale state by state

Add the US compliance pack — FTC Buyers Guide, federal odometer disclosure and per-state DMV title/registration — wire NMVTIS, Carfax & KBB, and a state-by-state sales-tax engine. Launch in a beachhead state, then expand.
